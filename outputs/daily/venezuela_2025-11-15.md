Venezuelan President Nicolás Maduro has condemned the United States' plans to conduct military exercises in Trinidad and Tobago, labeling them as "irresponsible." These drills are set to last five days and have heightened tensions between Venezuela and the United States. Maduro made these remarks during an address in Caracas, where supporters gathered to listen. In a related development, Maduro stated that Venezuela will not become "the Gaza of South America," in response to perceived threats from U.S. military activities in the Caribbean.

In economic news, Venezuela is seeking to strengthen its economic ties with India beyond their traditional oil-based relationship. Venezuelan Minister for Ecological Mining Development, Héctor Silva, met with India's Commerce and Industry Minister Piyush Goyal at the CII Partnership Summit in Visakhapatnam. The discussions focused on expanding cooperation in critical minerals and attracting Indian investment.

Additionally, the Venezuelan government has announced a slight increase in the "Bono Contra la Guerra Económica" for public sector workers, as part of ongoing social benefit programs.
